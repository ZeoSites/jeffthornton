  • How can drywall be repaired?

Customers from far and near have always asked questions about how they can restore drywalls by themselves without needing assistance from anyone, and our crew of professionals listed out the steps to follow for them.

For anyone yet to gain the knowledge of repairing drywalls, below is the roundup

  1. Clean hole with a blade knife. Cut at an angle so the exterior of the hole is greater than the interior.
  2. Fill the hole with a painter's putty. Ensure it level with the wall surface.
  3. Let it dry. Once completely dry, lightly sand the area until smooth.
  4. Spackle over the putty. You may need to
  5. For medium holes, use a drywall metal patch
  6. Sand the surface smooth around the hole
  7. Wipe off dust
  8. Peel paper backing off the patch. Firmly press patch in place with mesh facing outward.
  9. Spread drywall compound over the patch, feathering out the edges. Smooth out and let dry.
  10. Gently sand the surface until smooth with the wall. Repeat steps 9 and 10 until the patch can no longer be detected.
  11. Larger holes need patches made of drywall. Make sure it is the same thickness as the drywall already present
  12. Cut a square of drywall slightly larger than the hole. Score one side with a blade knife and snap it apart. Cut the back of the break line.
  13. Outline the patch around the hole using a pencil.
  14. Check for electrical cords and plumbing lines where you plan the cut.
  15. Use a drywall saw to cut out the drawn area.
  16. Screw in two wooden boards behind the drywall, one at the top and one at the bottom of the hole. This will keep the patch from falling through
  17. Screw the drywall patch to the wooden boards
  18. Spread drywall compound and add mesh
  19. Sand area and repeat steps 18 and 19 until the patch is undetectable.
  20. Paint over once it’s dry.

Tips for Maintaining and Caring for Drywalls

It is quite essential to preserve your drywalls looking their best and ensure their durability. You can successfully enforce this by maintaining and caring for your drywalls. Here are a list of tips to keep your drywalls in shape

Regular Cleaning

Clean out dust, dirt, and other debris that may have accumulated over time. Make use of a duster and depending on the design of your wall, maybe a mild detergent and water and gently remove this debris. Do not, under any circumstance, make use of harsh cleaning products as these are very bad for your drywalls.

Avoid Excessive Moisture

Your bathrooms and kitchens, most especially, are high in moisture levels. Ensure there is proper ventilation in these spaces to prevent moisture from penetrating the drywall. You can also make use of moisture resistance in these spaces.

Maintain the right humidity levels

Extreme humidity is risky for drywalls. Ensure your indoor humidity level is within the appropriate range for the prevention of moisture-related issues like mold or peeling paint.

Patch and Repair Regularly

As soon as you notice any form of damage to your drywall, ensure to patch and repair them immediately. Also, ensure a proper routine check and patch up damages if needed. Small issues can escalate to costly repairs if left unaddressed.

Protect Walls

Ensure your furniture is placed correctly to prevent scratches and bumps. For your chairs, tables, and other furniture, ensure you make use of furniture pads to avoid drywall damage.

Expert Maintenance

If the damage surpasses your DIY patching and repair capabilities, you can contact us at Jeff Thorton Drywall Specialist, Wailua Homesteads, HI, FL.. for a more expert approach and repair.

Inspect for Water Damage

Inspect your drywall regularly for any type of damage, be it discoloration, peeling paint, or soft spots caused by water leakages. If any, address all leaks as soon as possible.
